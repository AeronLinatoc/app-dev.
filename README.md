My Favorite Series
"Our Beloved Summer"
Our Beloved Summer is a South Korean romantic comedy-drama that was released between December 2021 and January 2022, written and directed by Lee Na-eun and Kim Yoon-jin, respectively. Choi Woo-shik stars as Choi Ung alongside Kim Da-mi as Kook Yeon-su, who previously collaborated on the film The Witch: Part 1. The Subversion.

Choi Ung and Kook Yeon-su are two ex-high school classmates with vastly different personalities he's a free spirit with an artistic flair, while she's serious-minded and ambitious. Although they constantly bickered throughout the production of a high school documentary, they fall head over heels in love. However, as life becomes increasingly complicated, their love life unravels and ends abruptly without a closure.

Years later, the documentary gets viral, and the film team decides to come back for a follow-up. Reluctantly, they reconnect. As they start filming again, so do they with an emotional journey packed with nostalgia, resentment, leftover feelings, and whether first love is worth a second chance.

The Turning Point: Seeing Each Other as Partners
One of the strongest moments is towards the end of the series, when Choi Ung and Kook Yeon-su sit down to have an open, honest conversation regarding their past and future. Finally, after all those years of miscommunication and emotional space between them, they discuss their fears openly  Yeon-su's fear of living in poverty and being a burden, and Ung's fear of abandonment and rejection.

In a soft, sincere moment, Yeon-su tells him she no longer wishes to go through life by herself. Ung responds that he's always dreamed of growing old with her. They both come to understand that love is not about healing each other, but about going through life in concert  as companions.

This moment is a turning point: they no longer view each other as high school sweethearts or failed love, but as equal friends, choosing one another with complete knowledge of their imperfections, their scars, and their love. It's raw, tender, and beautifully human.
